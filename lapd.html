About LAPD

The Los Angeles Police Department is one of the world's largest municipal law enforcement agencies, covering 468 square miles and serving over 4 million residents with professional sworn officers. Known for its specialized units, including the Air Support Division and SWAT team, the department focuses on community policing, reducing crime, and upholding constitutional principles. Within SCRP, officers maintain the highest standards of professionalism, conduct realistic patrol operations, and participate in dynamic emergency response scenarios that mirror real-world law enforcement challenges.

LAPD Divisions

🚔

Patrol Division

The backbone of LAPD operations. Patrol officers handle community calls, traffic stops, and general law enforcement duties throughout the city.

🚨

Traffic Enforcement

Specialized unit focused on traffic control, accident investigation, and highway safety operations across Los Angeles.

🔍

Detective Bureau

Investigative division handling major crimes, homicides, and complex investigations requiring specialized expertise.

🚁

Air Support

Helicopter operations providing aerial surveillance, traffic monitoring, and specialized support for ground units.

⚡

Special Operations

Elite tactical unit handling high-risk situations, armed robbery response, and specialized emergency operations.
